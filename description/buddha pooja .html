BUDDHA POOJA

Buddha Pooja, or Buddha Puja, is a ceremony of reverence and devotion honoring the Buddha in Buddhist traditions. It involves various rituals, prayers, and offerings conducted by Buddhist practitioners, typically in temples or monasteries.The ceremony often begins with the lighting of candles and incense, symbolizing enlightenment and purity. Buddhists may also offer flowers, fruits, and other symbolic items as a gesture of respect to the Buddha, his teachings (Dhamma), and the community of practitioners (Sangha)...

History about Buddha Pooja

"Buddha Pooja," or the worship of the Buddha, has deep roots in Buddhist history and tradition. It originated shortly after the passing of Siddhartha Gautama, the historical Buddha, around 483 BCE. After the Buddha's death, his disciples sought ways to honor his memory and teachings. This led to the development of rituals and ceremonies centered around venerating the Buddha, his relics, and the Bodhi tree under which he attained enlightenment. Over time, these practices evolved into formalized ceremonies known as "puja" or "pooja." Early Buddhists would gather in monasteries or sacred sites to perform rituals that included offering flowers, incense, lamps, and chanting of sutras in praise of the Buddha. As Buddhism spread across Asia, various cultures adopted and adapted these rituals, incorporating local customs and traditions. As a result, the forms of Buddha Pooja can vary widely depending on the region and Buddhist tradition.
