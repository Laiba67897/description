History about Asalha Pooja

Asalha Puja, also known as Dhamma Day, commemorates the Buddha's first sermon, the Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta, delivered to his first five disciples. It typically falls on the full moon day of the eighth lunar month. The sermon, given at Deer Park in Sarnath, India, marked the beginning of the Buddha's teachings on the Four Noble Truths and the Eightfold Path. Asalha Puja is one of the most important Theravada Buddhist festivals, celebrated with meditation, chanting, and offerings at temples worldwide. It signifies the establishment of the Sangha, the community of Buddhist monks, and the spread of the Buddha's teachings.

More about Asalha Pooja

Asalha Puja, also known as Dhamma Day, holds profound significance in the Theravada Buddhist tradition. It marks the anniversary of the Buddha's first sermon, often referred to as the "Turning of the Wheel of Dhamma" (Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta). This discourse, delivered at Deer Park in Sarnath, India, outlined the foundational principles of Buddhism—the Four Noble Truths and the Noble Eightfold Path. Celebrated on the full moon day of the eighth lunar month (typically July), Asalha Puja is a time for Buddhists to reflect on the teachings of the Buddha and their own spiritual path. It signifies the birth of the Sangha, the community of Buddhist monks, as the Buddha's first sermon led to the ordination of his first five disciples.
