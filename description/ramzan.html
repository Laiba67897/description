RAMADAN

Ramadan, also spelled as Ramadan, is the ninth month of the Islamic lunar calendar, observed by Muslims worldwide as a month of fasting, prayer, reflection, and community. Lasting for 29 or 30 days (depending on the lunar sighting), it commemorates the first revelation of the Quran to Prophet Muhammad, according to Islamic belief. During Ramzan, Muslims abstain from food, drink, smoking, and marital relations from dawn until sunset, focusing on spiritual growth, self-discipline, and charitable acts. It culminates in the joyous celebration of Eid al-Fitr, marked by feasting, prayers, and giving to those in need.

History about Ramadan

Ramadan, or Ramadan, has deep historical roots in Islam. It commemorates the month when the Quran, Islam's holy book, was first revealed to Prophet Muhammad by the Angel Gabriel. This revelation occurred during the Night of Power, a significant event in Islamic history. Ramzan is a time of spiritual reflection, self-discipline, and increased devotion to prayer and charity. Its observance includes fasting from dawn until sunset, abstaining from food, drink, smoking, and other physical needs. The month ends with the festive celebration of Eid al-Fitr, marking the completion of Ramzan and the beginning of Shawwal, the next lunar month in the Islamic calendar.

More about Ramadan

Ramadan, originating from the Arabic word "ramada," meaning scorching heat or dryness, holds profound significance in Islam. It symbolizes the period when the Quranic revelations commenced, emphasizing spiritual introspection, self-restraint, and compassion. Muslims worldwide engage in rigorous fasting from dawn till dusk, refraining not only from food and drink but also from negative behaviors. This sacred month fosters unity, empathy, and heightened devotion through increased prayers and charitable deeds. Eid al-Fitr, its culmination, signifies gratitude and community celebration, reinforcing bonds among believers. Ramzan's enduring legacy underscores Islam's core values of piety, generosity, and solidarity..
